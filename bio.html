Daniel Fromberg is a digital artist based in Chicago, IL, born in January 2000. He began writing music at age eight and recording it at age 10 on his brother's copy of Pro Tools and a four-track cassette recorder. He released two albums on the Teen River label in 2011 and subsequently performed at Chicago venues including The Empty Bottle, Township, The Burlington, Cafe Mustance, Permanent Records, and NYC's Cake Shop while in junior high. Daniel also performed on the public access variety show Chic-a-Go-Go twice and was profiled by Leor Galil for the Chicago Reader in 2014.


Following his early forays in the Chicago music scene, Daniel has explored film, literature, and criticism, and began collaborating with other young filmmakers in the city and environs. He is currently studying Visual and Critical Studies at the School of the Art Institute of Chicago.


Daniel now creates sound, moving image, and software art and is pursuing research in the discipline of sound studies, leading up to a thesis in 2022. He is working on original scores for two forthcoming feature films.
